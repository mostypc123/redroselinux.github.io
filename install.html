Installation Guide

To install Redrose Linux, you must first download a so-called disk image. This disk image will be about 1.5 to 2 gigabytes, which is less than a standard distro.

Next up, you have to flash the disk image into a drive. So take a USB drive, or whatever removable drive, and connect it to your current device.

On Windows: Download Rufus and follow instructions. All you have to do is select the disk image and the removable drive.

On Linux: run: sudo dd if=~/Downloads/redroselinux.iso of=/dev/sdX bs=4M status=progress && sync REPLACE /dev/sdX WITH YOUR REMOVABLE DRIVE!

Next, we will have to boot the USB. To do that, reboot your device, and try pressing these keys: Esc, Enter, F9, Del. This will get you into the BIOS/UEFI. If a menu with drives appeared, select your removable drive. If you see a menu of options, select Boot Menu or similar. From there, select your removable drive.

Now you have booted the Redrose Linux Installer! Wait until it boots, it will do everything on its own until the installer shows up.

Follow the installer prompts. Dictionary:

  • Username - name of the user
  • Root - Administrator
  • Console keymap - keyboard layout (us, uk, sk, etc.)
  • Timezone - your timezone in format Region/City. Example: Europe/Bratislava
  • Locale - language (like en_US.UTF-8)

From here on, the installer does everything by itself.
